REI KAWAKUBO

The Art of the In-Between

“The modes of expression that have always been important to me are fusion, imbalance, unfinished, elimination, and absence of intent."

    Rather than respond to trends, Kawakubo rooted her designs in concepts, straddling art and fashion. Her designs often looked voluminous on the wearer’s body. Because they did not fit the industry’s perception of what women wanted, her garments were sometimes described as antifashion.

  • 1942 born October 11, Tokyo, Japan
  • 1964 graduated in Fine arts and Aesthetics at Keio University in Tokyo
  • 1969 founded Comme des Garçons (CDG)
  • 1974 opened her first store, and within a decade she had 150 shops across Japan
  • 1981 opened her first Paris boutique that year following her outrageously successful debut on the Paris runway
  • 1983 opened her first shop in the U.S., in NYC
  • 1982 her influential collection, Destroy, featured oversized, loosely knit sweaters with holes that looked as though they had been slashed open. The dark, disheveled style was dubbed by the media the “postatomic look” or “Hiroshima chic”, or the “bag lady” look.
  • 1986 won the Fashion Group International award
  • 1986 exhibition “Mode et Photo, Comme des Garçons” at the Pompidou Centre in Paris
  • 1987 exhibition “Three Women: Madeleine Vionnet, Claire McCardell, and Rei Kawakubo” at the Fashion Institute of Technology in New York City
  • 1988 launched her own magazine, Six, a biannual large-format publication that displayed her seasonal collections. Intended as a reference to the sixth sense
  • 1993 she was honoured by the French government as a Chevalier in the Order of Arts and Letters
  • 1997 the collectionDress Meets Body, Body Meets Dress, which featured garments with lumps of padding positioned in unflattering places. It became known colloquially as the “lumps and bumps,” “tumor,” or “Quasimodo” collection and was criticized for blatantly disfiguring the female form. That collection inspired Kawakubo’s costume design for choreographer Merce Cunningham’s dance piece Scenario
  • 2000 won Excellence in Design Award from the Harvard University Graduate School of Design
  • 2004 CDG “guerrilla” stores, or “pop-ups,” brought CDG to cities around the globe on an ephemeral basis, lasting no longer than a year in any given location. Kawakubo, Joffe, and CDG are credited with having originated the pop-up store trend
  • 2008 exhibition “ReFusing Fashion: Rei Kawakubo” at the Museum of Contemporary Art Detroit
  • 2017 exhibition Rei Kawakubo/Comme des Garçons: Art of the In-Between” at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City
